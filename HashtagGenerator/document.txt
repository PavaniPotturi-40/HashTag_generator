
The article **“AI: The Good, The Bad and The Ugly”** discusses the positive and negative impacts of artificial intelligence on society. It explains that AI is neither completely good nor completely harmful; its effects depend on how humans choose to use it. ([Medium][1])

The **positive side of AI** includes significant improvements in areas such as healthcare, construction, and transportation. AI systems can help detect diseases like cancer or predict kidney failure using large datasets and advanced algorithms, which may save lives. In construction and engineering, AI can help design better buildings by optimizing factors like sunlight, wind, and environmental conditions. In transportation and logistics, AI can improve route planning, reduce traffic congestion, and increase efficiency. ([Medium][1])

However, the article also highlights the **negative aspects of AI**. Automation may replace certain jobs, leading to economic and social challenges. AI systems may also reinforce biases if they are trained on flawed data. Additionally, increased reliance on AI can create ethical issues and concerns about privacy and misuse of technology.

The **“ugly” side of AI** refers to more serious risks, such as the potential misuse of powerful technologies, increased inequality, and unintended consequences that could worsen global problems like climate change.

Overall, the article concludes that AI is a powerful tool whose impact depends on responsible human decisions and careful regulation. ([Medium][1])
